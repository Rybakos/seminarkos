Mars

Mars, often referred to as the "Red Planet" due to its reddish appearance, is the fourth planet from the Sun in our solar system.

It is a terrestrial planet with a diameter of about 6,779 kilometers, making it roughly half the size of Earth.

Olympus Mons

The surface of Mars is predominantly rocky and covered in a layer of reddish-brown dust and iron oxide, giving it a distinct reddish appearance. Mars has several notable surface features, including vast plains, impact craters, volcanoes, and deep canyons. The largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons, is located on Mars.

Mars has a thin atmosphere composed primarily of carbon dioxide (95.3%), with traces of nitrogen, argon, oxygen, and other gases. The atmospheric pressure on Mars is about 0.6% of Earth's surface pressure, making it significantly thinner. This thin atmosphere has a limited greenhouse effect, resulting in extreme temperature variations on the planet.

Mars has the largest volcano in the solar system called Olympus Mons. It is a shield volcano that stands about 25 kilometers high and has a diameter of approximately 600 kilometers, making it roughly three times taller than Mount Everest. Olympus Mons is considered one of the most impressive geological features in our solar system.
